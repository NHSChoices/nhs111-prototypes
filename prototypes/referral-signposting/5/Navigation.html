What if I feel worse?

If there are any new symptoms, or if the condition gets worse, changes or you have any other concerns, call 999 and ask for an ambulance

Advice for going to A&E

  • Do not drive yourself. Get someone else to take you.
  • Take all your current medicines with you.
  • If you have a mobile phone, take it with you

  1. Needlestick injury

Needlestick injury

  • Squeeze the wound to make it bleed.
  • Don't suck the wound.
  • Wash the wound thoroughly using soap and plenty of running water.
  • Don't scrub the wound, and don't use antiseptic or skin washes on it.
  • Dry with a paper or disposable towel.
  • Cover the wound with a waterproof plaster or dressing if you have one available.
  • If your skin, eyes or mouth have been splashed by something in a hypodermic needle, wash thoroughly with plenty of water.
  • Any needlestick injury needs to be checked by a healthcare professional to see if you need further treatment.
