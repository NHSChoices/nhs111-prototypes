What to do if I get worse

If there are any new symptoms, or if the condition gets worse, changes or you have any other concerns, call 111.
  1. Wound, head or neck
  2. Head injury
  1. Breathlessness, trauma
  2. Worsening, head injury

Wound, head or neck

A decision about whether tetanus immunisation is needed can be made when the injury is checked.

IF THE WOUND IS STILL BLEEDING:

Use a dry dressing or a clean cloth folded into a pad and press gently but firmly on the wound to stop the bleeding.

If the blood soaks through the pad don't take it off. Put another pad on top of it and keep pressing on the wound.

If helping someone else: try to keep their blood off your skin.

IF THE WOUND IS NOT BLEEDING:

Cover it with a plaster, a non-stick dressing or a non-fluffy clean cloth.

Head injury

A responsible adult should stay with anyone who has had a head injury to watch out for any problems.

Unless advised not to, or they have already been given, paracetamol can be used to relieve pain. Follow the instructions in the pack. If in doubt ask a pharmacist.

Don't use any other painkillers, they can cause complications following a head injury.

FOR ANY SWELLING:

Remove any jewellery or tight clothing from around the swelling.

Wrap an ice pack (or bag of frozen peas) in a cloth and put it on the area for 15-20 minutes. Repeat every 2-3 hours when awake.

Don't put ice directly on the skin. Don't leave ice on while asleep.

Breathlessness, trauma

If they are able to, allow them to adopt a comfortable position.

If possible, support their back or hold them in an upright position.

Worsening, head injury

If they cannot be woken up or they become dazed, groggy or have a fit - DIAL 999.

If there are any problems understanding, speaking, balancing or walking - DIAL 999.

If there is any loss of feeling in any part of the body, general weakness or new deafness - DIAL 999.

If there is any vomiting, any change in vision, or a severe or worsening headache - GO STRAIGHT TO THE NEAREST EMERGENCY DEPARTMENT.

If there are any new symptoms, or if the condition gets worse, changes or you have any other concerns call back on 999.
